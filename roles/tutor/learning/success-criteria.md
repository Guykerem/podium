# Private Tutor — Success Criteria

How to evaluate whether each skill is performing well, and how to evaluate the tutor role overall.

## research-loop

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | New high-quality sources (CRAAP ≥ 4) enter memory/sources/ on every sweep. Duplicates are merged. Tags link sources to concepts. No low-quality sources make it through. Student finds the material genuinely useful. |
| **Okay** | Sources enter, but some are marginal or off-topic. Tagging is inconsistent. Dedup works most of the time. |
| **Poor** | Sources are stale, off-topic, or duplicates. Quality scoring is miscalibrated. Student ignores what the agent finds. |

## synthesize

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Synthesis is calibrated to the student's level — not too basic, not too technical. Cites primary sources. Surfaces conflicting claims honestly. Student can use the synthesis to answer a question they couldn't before. |
| **Okay** | Synthesis is accurate but generic. Calibration is imperfect. Citations are present but the student rarely follows them. |
| **Poor** | Synthesis hallucinates, miscites, or loses the student. Wrong complexity tier. Student has to re-read the primary sources anyway. |

## learning-plan

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Plan has clear milestones, respects prerequisites, and leads toward the student's stated goal. Review cadence matches the mastery model. Student can explain where they are and why. |
| **Okay** | Plan exists but milestones are fuzzy. Prerequisite logic has gaps. Student follows it but doesn't own it. |
| **Poor** | Plan is arbitrary, skips prerequisites, or chases content rather than goals. Student drifts or abandons. |

## quiz

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Questions are unambiguous, well-distributed across Bloom's levels, with plausible distractors. Difficulty matches current mastery. Results meaningfully update the mastery model. Student says "that was a good question." |
| **Okay** | Questions are accurate but cluster at one Bloom's level (usually "remember"). Distractors are sometimes implausible. Calibration is loose. |
| **Poor** | Questions leak answers, have ambiguous wording, or are misaligned with what's been taught. Mastery model is unaffected or updated wrongly. |

## assess-progress

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Report surfaces specific gaps with evidence (which quiz items, which interactions). Identifies retention trends. Recommends concrete next steps. Student reads it and acts. |
| **Okay** | Report is accurate at high level but vague on specifics. Student skims and moves on. |
| **Poor** | Report is generic, confirmation-biased, or miscalibrated ("you're doing great!" when they're not). Student stops trusting assessments. |

## podcast-pipeline

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Podcasts target real gaps. Source bundles are coherent (not a grab-bag). Student listens and gains understanding they didn't have before. Pipeline is reliable. |
| **Okay** | Podcasts are generated but targeting is loose. Student listens sometimes. |
| **Poor** | Podcasts are off-topic, duplicative, or the pipeline breaks silently. Student doesn't listen. |

## adapt-style

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Good** | Observed preferences diverge from declared ones in interesting ways, and the agent adapts. Retention per format improves over time. Student says "you figured out how I learn." |
| **Okay** | Agent tracks formats but doesn't meaningfully adapt. Declared preferences dominate regardless of evidence. |
| **Poor** | Agent over-adapts to noise, locks into one format prematurely, or ignores clear signals of disengagement. |

## Overall Role Success

The tutor is succeeding if, after four weeks, the student:

1. **Has measurable mastery progression** — average retrievability across active concepts is rising on spaced retests, not just immediate ones
2. **Shows transfer** — can apply taught concepts to problems not seen during teaching (measured via held-out quiz items)
3. **Is calibrated** — self-reported confidence correlates (> 0.6) with actual quiz performance
4. **Owns their path** — can articulate the goal, current milestone, and next milestone without prompting
5. **Sustains engagement** — session cadence matches the agreed rhythm; abandoned-session rate < 15%

## Anti-Patterns the Role Must Avoid

- **Coverage theater.** Touching many concepts without mastery checks — looks productive, isn't.
- **Praise inflation.** "Great job!" on a wrong answer. Erodes calibration and trust.
- **Path drift.** Letting the student follow curiosity so far from the plan that the goal recedes.
- **Quiz-as-grading.** Treating quizzes as summative when they should be formative learning instruments.
- **Format monoculture.** Defaulting to one format regardless of evidence.
- **Source hoarding.** Accumulating material the student will never consume.
- **Silent autonomy creep.** Escalating to autonomy L3 without earning it.

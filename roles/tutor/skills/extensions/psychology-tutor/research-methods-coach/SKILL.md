# Research Methods Coach

Psychology research methods is a wide terrain — design, measurement, statistics, ethics — and students often fail to connect them. This skill keeps them linked: a design choice has measurement consequences; a measurement choice has stats consequences; a stats choice has ethical consequences.

## How It Works

### 1. Start with the Question
Every methods coaching session begins: "what's the research question, in one sentence?" If the student can't state it, the methods conversation is premature — work on the question first.

### 2. Work Across the Four Axes
For any study (student's or published):
1. **Design** — experimental, quasi, correlational, qualitative. What the design can and can't tell you.
2. **Measurement** — operationalizations, validity (construct, internal, external), reliability, measurement bias.
3. **Statistics** — appropriate tests, assumptions, effect sizes, power, multiple-comparison control.
4. **Ethics** — consent, deception, harm minimization, justice, ongoing consent, data protection.

Each axis has its own mastery concepts (IV manipulation, Cronbach's α, ANOVA assumptions, IRB protocol). But the teaching principle is that choices on one axis constrain the others — surface the links, not just the content.

### 3. Worked Examples of Bad Designs
High-value teaching: show a flawed study, let the student find the flaw before you name it. Build a library of famous flawed studies (Bobo doll demand characteristics, original Milgram IRB issues, small-n priming claims, etc.) in `roles/tutor/knowledge/research-methods/bad-designs.md`.

### 4. Trigger Ethics Checkpoints
Any time the student proposes a study involving human subjects, run the **ethics checkpoint**:
- Consent procedure?
- Risk to participants (physical, psychological, data)?
- Vulnerable populations?
- Deception justified and debriefed?
- IRB-required?

Even if the study is hypothetical, the checkpoint is a skill-mastery event.

### 5. Critique Published Studies
Pair with `case-study-analysis` for real papers. Student applies the four-axis framework to a published study and produces a structured critique.

### 6. Stats Scaffolding
When stats are the weak axis: use worked examples (data → assumption check → test → interpretation). Interleave once mastery rises. Don't let stats become a formula-memorization slog — connect every formula to the question it answers.

## Composition
- Composes `synthesize` (explain constructs), `quiz` (concept retrieval), `case-study-analysis` (apply to published work).

## Autonomy Behavior
- **L1** — Each axis prompt approved before delivery; ethics checkpoint manual.
- **L2** — Routine coaching; ethics checkpoint still surfaces as an explicit check.
- **L3** — Full loop; ethics checkpoint remains non-silent (always shown to student).

## Activation
Extension. Activate for psychology-methods curricula, thesis/dissertation work, or any session critiquing research. Don't activate for pure clinical or pure theory work.

# Grammar Drill

Grammar-in-context first, drill to automate. A form is introduced through comprehensible input — a story, a scenario, a conversation — *then* drilled to move it from effortful to automatic. Drilling without context produces form-knowers who can't speak.

## How It Works

### 1. Introduce in Context
Before any drill, the student encounters the form at least once in comprehensible text, dialogue, or audio. Drilling a form the student hasn't heard used is pure pattern-matching — no transfer.

### 2. Three Drill Types
Progress through:
1. **Contextualized cloze** — blanks in a paragraph/dialogue. Student fills the form. Low cognitive load.
2. **Transformation** — convert a given sentence (present → past, active → passive, statement → question). Requires productive manipulation.
3. **Guided production** — prompt requires producing the form in a fresh sentence ("tell me what you would do if…").

Accuracy thresholds: 80% before advancing cloze → transformation → production.

### 4. Interleave Across Forms
Blocked drill (same form 20 times) feels good but decays fast. After initial acquisition, **interleave** multiple related forms in the same drill set (all tenses, all pronoun cases, etc.). Interleaving trains discrimination — the skill of picking the right form in context.

### 5. Tie to SRS
Each form is a concept in `learning-plan`. Drill items become SRS cards scheduled by SM-2/FSRS. Review intervals expand as accuracy stabilizes.

### 6. Flag Fossilized Errors
If an error persists > 10 drills on a form, it's not a slip — it's a miscalibrated mental model. Stop drilling, switch to `synthesize` for a targeted explanation of the underlying rule, then drill again.

### 7. Log and Graph
Track per-form accuracy over time. Surface in weekly review: which forms are becoming automatic, which are resisting.

## Composition
- Composes `quiz` (delivery mechanism), `learning-plan` (SRS scheduling), `synthesize` (rule explanation on fossilized errors).
- Feeds `conversation-practice` (drilled forms become producible).

## Autonomy Behavior
- **L1** — Drill set and items proposed per session.
- **L2** — Routine drilling; agent surfaces fossilized-error flags.
- **L3** — Full SRS loop; weekly trends reported.

## Activation
Extension. Core for language learning; activate alongside `conversation-practice` once forms have been introduced.

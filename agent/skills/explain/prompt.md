# Explain Skill

## What This Skill Does

Breaks down a concept into clear, accessible language using analogies from cognitive science and psychology. Can produce text explanations, visual diagrams (described for rendering), or structured comparisons.

## When To Use

- Student encounters a new technical concept (agentic loop, RAG, embeddings, etc.)
- Student asks "what does X mean?" or "how does X work?"
- Student is confused by something from the lecture or readings
- Student wants to explain a concept to someone else and needs help structuring it

## How It Works

1. **Identify what they already know** — ask or infer from context what related concepts are familiar
2. **Find the cognitive bridge** — map the technical concept to a psychological or cognitive analogy
3. **Explain in layers:**
   - One sentence: the simplest possible version
   - One paragraph: the key mechanisms
   - Deep dive (if requested): how it actually works technically
4. **Ground with an example** — a concrete scenario the student can relate to
5. **Test understanding** — ask the student to explain it back in their own words or apply it to a new case

## Autonomy Behavior

- **Level 1:** Always ask what they already know first. Check understanding after explaining.
- **Level 2:** Infer prior knowledge from context. Check understanding for complex topics only.
- **Level 3:** Explain directly at the depth that fits the conversation. Check only if there are signs of confusion.

## Cognitive Analogy

This skill mirrors **teaching and metacognition** — the ability to take knowledge from one domain and translate it so it makes sense in another. It's what a great tutor does: not dumbing down, but finding the bridge between what you know and what you're learning.
